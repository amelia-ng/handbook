# L1: Taxonomy of Driving Automation

## 1. Basic Definition

- **Driving task**
    - **Perceiving the environment**: вЂ‹This includes tracking a car's motion in вЂ‹identifying the various elements in the world around us, вЂ‹like the road surface, вЂ‹road signs, vehicles, pedestrians and so on. вЂ‹We also need to track all moving objects and predict their future motions. 
    - **Motion planning**: Planning how to reach from point A to point B
    - **Vehicle control**: take the appropriate *steering, вЂ‹break* and *acceleration decisions* to вЂ‹control the vehicle's *position and velocity* on the road.
- **Operational Design Domain (ODD)**: 
    - The ODD constitutes the *operating conditions* вЂ‹under which a given system is designed to function.
    - вЂ‹It includes environmental, time of day, вЂ‹roadway and other characteristics under which the car will perform reliably.
    - вЂ‹Clearly defining the operating conditions for which a self-driving car is designed, вЂ‹is crucial to ensuring the safety of the system. вЂ‹So the ODD needs to be planned out carefully in advance.

## 2. Requirements for Driving Automation

- **Driver Attention** requirement:
    - For example, can you watch a movie while driving to work? вЂ‹Or do you need to keep your attention on the steering wheel at all times?
    - Driver attention is one of the crucial questions вЂ‹to consider when defining the level of autonomy. 

- **Driver Action** requirement:
    - How much driver action is actually needed?
    - For example do you need to steer? вЂ‹Does the car take care of the speed or do you control that as well? вЂ‹Do you need to change lanes or can вЂ‹the car stay in the current lane without any intervention? вЂ‹What exactly do we need to expect when we say that the car can automatically drive? 

## 3. What makes up a driving task?

- **Lateral control**:

    вЂ‹The task of steering and navigating laterally on the road. вЂ‹Turning left, right, going straight or tracking a curve and so on.

- **Longitudinal control**:

    вЂ‹This is the task where we control the position or velocity of the car along the roadway, вЂ‹through actions like breaking or acceleration. 

- **Object and Event Detection and Response (OEDR)**: 
    - OEDR is essentially the ability to detect objects and events вЂ‹that immediately affect the driving task and to react to them appropriately.
    - вЂ‹OEDR really encompasses a large portion of autonomous driving. So, is used in conjunction with вЂ‹the specific Operational Design Domain to categorize current self-driving systems.

- **Planning**:

    вЂ‹As immediate response is already part of OEDR, вЂ‹planning is primarily concerned with the long and short term plans needed to travel to вЂ‹a destination or execute maneuvers such as lean changes and intersection crossings. 

- **Miscellaneous**:
    
    Actions like signaling with indicators, вЂ‹hand-waving, interacting with other drivers and so on.

## 4. Taxonomy for driving automation

Based on [SAE: J3016_201806 - Taxonomy and Definitions for Terms Related to Driving Automation Systems for On-Road Motor Vehicles](https://www.sae.org/standards/j3016_201806-taxonomy-definitions-terms-related-driving-automation-systems-road-motor-vehicles)

**Autonomous Capabilities**

1) Can this system handle steering tasks or lateral control? вЂ‹

2) Can it perform acceleration, вЂ‹braking and velocity manipulation tasks or longitudinal control?

3) Can the system perform object and event detection and response and to what degree?

4) Can the system handle emergency situations by вЂ‹itself or does it always need a driver to be attentive during emergencies?

5) Can the system perform in all scenarios and all conditions? вЂ‹Or does it have a limited ODD or set of operating conditions that it can handle safely?

вЂ‹Based on these questions let's walk through the commonly-used levels of вЂ‹automation defined by the SAE Standard J3 016

### Level 0: No Automation

- Full human perception, вЂ‹planning and control 
- вЂ‹There is no driving automation вЂ‹whatsoever and everything is done by the driver.
- вЂ‹If an autonomous system assist the driver by вЂ‹performing either lateral or longitudinal control tasks, вЂ‹we are in level one autonomy.

## 5. Limitations of this taxonomy
