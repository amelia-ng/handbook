## 2. Industrial Robots

### 2.1. Robots in manufacturing
a. Articulated robot

* Multi-degree-of freedom joints
* Perform tasks: Precision welding, High-speed painting, and Automated assembly

b. SCARA (Selective Compliance Articulated Robot Arm)

 * Ideal for precise pick-and-place operations

c. Delta robot

* Parallel-link mechanisms
* Excel in high-speed sorting and packaging

d. Cartesian robot

* Precision Task
* Linear movements along corthogonal axes
    - Crucial for CNC machining and additive manufacturing
    - Providing high precision and reliability

### 2.2. Mobile Robots

A variety of autonomous systems designed to navigate diverse environment

a. Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs) & Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs)

- Revolutionizing logistics and warehouse management
    * Advanced path planning
    * Real-time navigation

b. Drones or Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs)

- Utilize real-time sensor fusion and computer vision algorithms: Aerial surveying, Geospatial mapping, and Precision delivery.

c. Legged Robots (e.g: quadrupeds)

- Designed for rugged terrain
- Superior stability & adaptability in exploratory missions

d. Underwater Robots

- ROVs (Remotely operated vehicles) and UAVs (Unmanned Aerial Vehicles)
- Sonar and advanced imaging technologies: Deep-sea exploration and Subsea infrastructure inspections.

### 2.3. Healthcare Robotics
- Surgical robots: 
    * Minimally invasive procedures with high precisions
- Rehabilitation robots
    * Utilizing real-time biomechanical modeling
    * Provide adaptive support during physical therapy

### 2.4. Hospitality Robotics
- Room service and guest interaction
- Leverage natural language processing and gesture recognition

### 2.5. Security Robotics
- Enhance safety through autonomous patrolling and real-time surveillance
- Equipped with AI-driven anomaly detection for prompt threat response

### 2.6. Humanoid Robots
- Mimic human biomechanics and social behaviors
- Research, education, and human-robot interaction (HRI)

### 2.7. Bipedal and Social Robots
- Bipedal robots
    * Emulate human locomotion
- Exoskeletons
    * Augment human physical capabilities
- Social robots:
    * Advanced emotion recognition & interactive AI to engage with humans
- Telepresence robots:
    * Enable virtual presence and interaction in remote locations: Utilize real-time video, audio, and environment feedback
    * Bridge physical distances in: Education, health care, and business

### 2.8. Soft Robots
- Constructed from compliant materials
- Designed for safe human interaction & adaptability in unstructured environments.
- Flexibility and safety features make them ideal for tasks in close proximity to humans.
- Employing pneumatic actuation and bio-inspired designs.

### 2.9. Swarm Robots
- Distributed intelligence: Mutiple robots work together to achieve collective goals
- Inspired by the behavior of social insects like ants
